## F2 - Free Group on 2 Generators

### Mathematical Description
The free group F₂ = ⟨a, b⟩ is generated by two elements with no relations other than the group axioms. Words reduce to the identity only through free reduction (cancellation of adjacent inverse pairs like `aA` or `Bb`).

### Alphabet
| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| `a` | Generator a |
| `A` | Inverse of a (a⁻¹) |
| `b` | Generator b |
| `B` | Inverse of b (b⁻¹) |

### Identity Test
A word equals the identity if and only if it freely reduces to the empty word by repeatedly canceling adjacent inverse pairs.

## Z2 - Free Abelian Group on 2 Generators

### Mathematical Description
The free abelian group Z² = ⟨a, b | ab = ba⟩ is generated by two commuting elements. Words equal the identity when the total count of each generator equals its inverse count.

### Alphabet
| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| `a` | Generator a |
| `A` | Inverse of a (a⁻¹) |
| `b` | Generator b |
| `B` | Inverse of b (b⁻¹) |

For the anbn variant: only `a` and `b` (lowercase) are used.

### Identity Test
A word equals the identity if and only if:
- count(a) = count(A) AND count(b) = count(B)
- For anbn variant: count(a) = count(b)

## B3 - Braid Group on 3 Strands

### Mathematical Description
The braid group B₃ has generators σ₁ and σ₂ with the braid relation: σ₁σ₂σ₁ = σ₂σ₁σ₂. This group describes braiding patterns of 3 strands.

### Alphabet
| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| `s1` | Generator σ₁ |
| `S1` | Inverse σ₁⁻¹ |
| `s2` | Generator σ₂ |
| `S2` | Inverse σ₂⁻¹ |

### Identity Test
A word equals the identity if it reduces to the trivial braid using the braid relations and free reductions. Computationally verified using braid normal forms.

## B4 - Braid Group on 4 Strands

The braid group B₄ has generators σ₁, σ₂, σ₃ with relations:

This group describes braiding patterns of 4 strands.

### Alphabet

## S4 - Symmetric Group on 4 Elements

### Mathematical Description
The symmetric group S₄ consists of all permutations of 4 elements. Generated by adjacent transpositions with relations:
- sᵢ² = 1 (each generator is its own inverse)
- sᵢsⱼ = sⱼsᵢ when |i-j| ≥ 2
- sᵢsᵢ₊₁sᵢ = sᵢ₊₁sᵢsᵢ₊₁

### Alphabet
| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| `s1` | Transposition (1 2) |
| `s2` | Transposition (2 3) |
| `s3` | Transposition (3 4) |

**Note:** No inverse symbols needed since sᵢ = sᵢ⁻¹ in symmetric groups.

### Identity Test
A word equals the identity if the composed permutation is the identity permutation. This is verified by computing the resulting permutation.
